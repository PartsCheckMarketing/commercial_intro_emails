4How to add your signature in Outlook

One-time setup. Once saved, every new email and reply you send will include it automatically.

  1. Click Copy signature on the version you want above.
  2. Classic Outlook (desktop): File › Options › Mail › Signatures › New, name it PartsCheck, click in the edit box and press Ctrl+V.
    New Outlook / Outlook on the web: Settings (gear icon) › Accounts › Signatures › New signature, paste with Ctrl+V.
  3. Type over the [Full Name], [Job Title], [firstname.lastname]@partscheck.net.au and (if applicable) [04XX XXX XXX] fields with your details.
  4. Under Choose default signature, set PartsCheck as the default for New messages and Replies/forwards.
  5. Click Save. Close and reopen Outlook if it doesn't appear immediately.

5How to test your signature

  1. Open a new email in Outlook and confirm the signature appears automatically with your name, title and email correct.
  2. Send a test email to yourself (harry.robertson@partscheck.net.au can be CC'd if you want a second pair of eyes). Open it and check the logo loads, the green divider line is visible, and the links are clickable.
  3. Reply to any existing email and check the signature still appears (some Outlook versions hide signatures on replies until you switch on the default).
  4. Open the test on your phone in Outlook mobile and confirm the layout doesn't break and the logo still loads.
  5. If anything looks off, repeat step 4. Most issues are caused by the signature being pasted as plain text. Use Ctrl+V, not "Paste as text".
